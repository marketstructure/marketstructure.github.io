China's regulator is expected to step up oversight to prevent similar governance failures.​
  • In Taiwan, Cathay Life Insurance announced plans to gradually mandate all of its NT$7 trillion ($228 billion) in investment assets to parent group subsidiary Cathay Securities Investment Trust, as Taiwanese authorities push to build the island into a regional wealth management hub. The move is part of a broader government initiative encouraging major insurers to allocate more mandates to domestic asset managers to strengthen Taiwan's asset management industry.
  • Yuexiu Enterprises completed its acquisition of Hong Kong Life Insurance on October 9, becoming the 100% ultimate owner after receiving regulatory approval on September 25. The transaction involved Yuexiu acquiring an 83.33% stake through its subsidiary, with the remaining 16.67% held through its existing subsidiary Chong Hing Insurance. Hong Kong Life, established in 2001, offers life, critical illness, savings, investment-linked, and medical insurance products and has over 100,000 policyholders.
  • The Asian Development Bank approved a $400 million policy-based loan for the Philippines' Insurance Reform Program Subprogram 1, marking ADB's first dedicated insurance reform program for the country. The initiative will be implemented in three sequenced subprograms in partnership with the Insurance Commission, focusing on modernizing regulation and supervision, enhancing digitalization and climate finance, and increasing long-term credit intermediation for government infrastructure projects. The program aims to create a more efficient, consumer-oriented, and technologically advanced insurance market while strengthening climate risk management and disaster resilience.​

The measure, previously limited to policies covering the policyholder or spouse, aims to make family insurance more affordable and encourage financial preparedness for dependents.
