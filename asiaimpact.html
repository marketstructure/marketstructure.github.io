Asia Sustainable Investing: Weekly Digest – October 21, 2025
This is an experimental briefing generated by an automated AI workflow with limited human oversight. Please verify critical information independently.
  • On October 20, the Asian Development Bank and World Bank signed a $3 billion sovereign exposure exchange agreement—ADB’s first with the World Bank and sixth overall—to lower portfolio concentration risk, thereby freeing up capital under ADB’s limits framework and boosting its lending headroom from developing member countries. This strategic collaboration directly supports sustainable finance by enabling ADB to expand climate mitigation and adaptation lending, including renewable energy, resilient infrastructure, and low-carbon transport projects, helping countries close financing gaps while advancing their net-zero and Sustainable Development Goal commitments.
  • India draft plan reveals $21 trillion net-zero investment requirement (Oct 17): Government plan outlines funding needed to achieve net-zero emissions by 2070 while lifting population from poverty, with peak emissions targeted for 2045—a decade earlier than current trajectory. The strategy envisions renewables constituting 65% of energy mix by 2070, nuclear at 11%, and coal declining to 4% from 49% in 2020.​
  • Indonesia reopens carbon market to foreign buyers (Oct 15): Presidential decree signed Oct 10 permits international trade in carbon offset units after a four-year moratorium, requiring verification by accredited bodies using international standards. The policy reversal enables credits from Indonesian projects to be sold to voluntary offset buyers while contributing to Indonesia's own climate targets.​
  • ICVCM opens first physical office in Singapore (Oct 16): The global carbon market watchdog established its first Asia Pacific hub in partnership with Singapore EDB, marking the organization's first physical presence after previously operating entirely remotely. The office will focus on developing methodologies for regionally-relevant solutions including transition credits from early coal plant retirement and peatland restoration projects.​
  • Bursa Malaysia-Malaysia Forest Fund partnership (Oct 15): Two-year collaboration established to advance carbon market development through potential inclusion of Forest Conservation Certificates in ESG-themed indices and promotion of the Forest Carbon Offset national crediting programme launching next year. The partnership will explore trading FCO units on Bursa Carbon Exchange to boost market liquidity for domestic and international participants.​
  • Asia Climate Philanthropy Advisory launches as first regional platform (Oct 13-16): Founded and led from Asia with Laura Lee as founding head, the non-profit initiative addresses the region's $815 billion annual climate finance gap. The advisory is backed by Tara Climate Foundation, Children's Investment Fund Foundation, Asia Philanthropy Circle, and other foundations to mobilize catalytic philanthropic capital.​
  • BlueOrchard reports Japanese clients seeking overseas impact investments (Oct 17): Assets under management from Japanese investors at the Schroders Group firm expanded more than fourfold over three years to ¥150 billion ($998 million), accounting for roughly one-fifth of global impact investments. GPIF published its 2024 Sustainability Investment Report (Oct 15) and is studying the relationship between impact investing and investment returns.​
