  • China approves first wholly foreign-owned insurance asset management firms in Shanghai: AIA and Aegon Group received regulatory approval to establish asset management companies in Shanghai, marking the first such approvals in China's financial capital. AIA will establish its company through mainland subsidiary AIA Life Insurance, while Aegon plans direct investment funding, following similar moves by Allianz (2021) and Prudential Financial (February 2025).
  • Retail hedge fund access expands: BlackRock launched its Systematic Asia Pacific Equity Absolute Return Fund for retail investors in Singapore and Hong Kong, featuring $727 million in assets, 2,800+ holdings, and 23.7% returns in 2024 through market-neutral long/short strategy. The democratization continues BlackRock's broader push into alternative strategy retail distribution.
  • Taiwan enhances institutional climate investing: Bureau of Labor Funds appointed BlackRock, HSBC Global Asset Management, State Street Global Advisors Singapore, and UBS Asset Management Singapore for a $1.6 billion global climate action passive equity mandate benchmarked against the MSCI World Climate Action Index. The five-year mandate represents significant institutional commitment to ESG-aligned strategies.
  • Asia-Pacific PE fundraising: Bain Capital is raising $2 billion for its second Japan mid-market fund, targeting a combined $9 billion across latest buyout funds including $7 billion for Bain Capital Asia Fund VI. Separately, Advent International is preparing to launch a dedicated Asia-Pacific private equity fund targeting up to $3 billion, marking renewed regional focus after winding down its Japan fund in 2008.

Sovereign Wealth Funds

  • Indonesia-Singapore collaboration framework established: Indonesian President Prabowo Subianto invited Temasek Holdings to become a strategic partner for newly established sovereign wealth fund Danantara, focusing on renewable energy and sustainable industrial zones development in Batam, Bintan, and Karimun. Danantara, launched February 2025, potentially manages assets worth over $1 trillion.
  • Regional sovereign funds join AI infrastructure consortium: Singapore's Temasek and Kuwait Investment Authority joined the AI Infrastructure Partnership backed by Microsoft, BlackRock, and MGX, targeting initial investment over $30 billion with ambitions to mobilize up to $100 billion for US AI infrastructure projects. The consortium also includes Nvidia and Elon Musk's xAI startup.
  • Oman establishes second Vietnam-focused fund: Oman Investment Authority signed agreements to establish the Vietnam New Era Growth Fund with initial capital of $200 million, doubling down on the Southeast Asian market following its first $400 million Vietnam-Oman Investment Fund launched in 2008.
