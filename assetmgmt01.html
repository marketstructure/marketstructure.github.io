  • Strategic Consolidation of Asia Asset Management Platform. Temasek-owned Seviora Group announced the integration of Pavilion Capital, creating a leading Asia-based asset management group with combined assets under management of approximately $72 billion. Pavilion, which specializes in Asia-focused private equity fund of funds and co-investment strategies, will retain its brand while marketing products under Seviora's platform, with integration expected by Q1 2026. This move strengthens Temasek's primary asset management platform as it pursues its most significant structural reorganization in over a decade, positioning the combined entity to better capture institutional demand for Asia's growth opportunities and diversify investment solutions across private equity, private credit, and public markets.​
  • Asia PE Fundraising Surge. Asia's private equity markets are experiencing a marked acceleration as two of the region's largest dealmakers simultaneously launched outsized fundraising campaigns. Hillhouse Investment initiated a $7 billion fundraise for its sixth Asia-focused private equity fund, while KKR kicked off fundraising for its fifth Asia platform targeting $15 billion. Both efforts represent significant tests of institutional investor appetite as deal conditions improve across the region, driven by reopened IPO exit channels and renewed investor interest in Asia—particularly China, where valuations have attracted capital shifting away from saturated U.S. markets. Hillhouse's previous $18 billion fundraise in 2021 remains Asia's record, and the current campaign reflects broader confidence in the sector's revival after years of cautious capital deployment.​
  • Significant Manager Selections. South Korea's National Pension Service (NPS) selected four domestic equity managers on November 27 to oversee mandates across two distinct strategies. KCGI Asset Management and Mirae Asset Global Investments secured "Long-Term Growth" mandates targeting structural market changes and stable long-term alpha generation, while KB Asset Management and NH-Amundi Asset Management won "Small & Mid-Cap Equity" mandates focused on Korean small-cap and KOSDAQ 150 securities. Meanwhile, Japan's GPIF awarded a significant global equity mandate to Nordea Asset Management for its BetaPlus Enhanced Global ex-Japan Equity Strategy, reflecting institutional preference for low-tracking-error, quantitative frameworks delivering consistent alpha with disciplined risk controls.
  • Landmark India Asset Manager IPO. ICICI Prudential Asset Management received regulatory approval from India's SEBI on November 26 to launch a $1.2 billion initial public offering, targeting the second week of December with anticipated listing by December 19, 2025. The offering represents a pure offer for sale (OFS) by the company's 49% owner, UK-listed Prudential, which will divest its 10% stake at a targeted $12–$12.5 billion valuation. ICICI Bank, the 51% shareholder, intends to retain its majority position while acquiring up to an additional 2% from Prudential ahead of the listing, marginally increasing its stake. As India's largest asset manager by active mutual fund quarterly average assets under management (13.3% market share as of March 31, 2025), ICICI Prudential manages approximately $112 billion in total assets and posted net profit growth of 29.3% year-over-year to 26.6 billion rupees ($298 million) for fiscal year 2025, driven by rising fee and commission income.
