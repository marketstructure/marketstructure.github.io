With Ovata returning capital from a fund managing about $1.5bn, the development matters less as an isolated closure than as further evidence that scale, capital backing and platform economics are continuing to pull Asian equity trading talent toward global multi-strategy firms.

Korea Post has launched a tender for a four‑year advisory mandate on a 2.74 trillion won (US$1.77bn) foreign bond portfolio for its savings and insurance units, underlining continued outsourcing of global fixed-income expertise. Korea Finance for Construction is simultaneously seeking a manager for a 500 billion won domestic private equity blind pool targeting an 8% IRR, with bidders required to co‑invest at least 2%. In parallel, NPS has added a “client communication” criterion that explicitly favours external managers with operations in its Jeonju headquarters, tilting future Korean equity and bond mandates toward locally present firms.

KB Asset Management signed a strategic MOU with ChinaAMC in Seoul on June 22 to jointly develop ETFs linked to the Korean and Hong Kong markets, coordinate listings in Korea and Hong Kong, and develop joint indices for new products. The significance is that the tie-up is not just a distribution agreement but an attempt to build a cross-border product pipeline around index creation, exchange listings and ETF Connect, giving Korean and Chinese managers a more formal route to package regional exposures for offshore investors.

  • China tightens TRS-based offshore access for wealthy investors. Chinese regulators have told domestic brokerages to stop expanding offshore-linked total return swap books, suspending new contracts and additions to existing mandates while allowing outstanding positions to remain in place. The immediate significance is that another informal route for mainland money to reach offshore equities is being constrained, which modestly raises the relative importance of formal cross-border fund structures and regulated distribution channels for managers serving Chinese demand.
