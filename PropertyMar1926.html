  • China's 15th Five-Year Plan Reorients Property Sector — China released its 2026–2030 Five-Year Plan on March 13, dedicating an entire chapter to the real estate sector for the first time, with structural reform explicitly prioritised over growth-driven expansion. Key provisions include mandatory transition to completed-home sales (effectively ending the pre-sale model), broader housing provident fund coverage, accelerated urban village renovation, and targeted inventory reduction. The plan categorises real estate alongside local government debt and small financial institutions as areas of systemic structural risk requiring active resolution.
  • Shanghai cuts commercial property down payments – Shanghai’s central bank branch has lowered the minimum down payment on loans for commercial-use and mixed commercial‑residential properties from 50% to 30%, effective 16 March, in the city’s first change to commercial property lending rules in over a decade. The move implements a national policy announced in January lowering the nationwide floor for such mortgages to “no less than 30%.” Local officials framed the adjustment as supporting inventory clearance and aligning with the new real‑estate development model.
  • Swire Properties Books HK$1.5B Statutory Loss Despite Record Underlying Profit — Swire Properties reported full-year 2025 results on March 12, posting a statutory loss attributable to shareholders of approximately HK$1.5 billion (US$196 million), driven by a full-year fair value loss on investment properties significantly exceeding the HK$4.68 billion loss already recorded in H1 alone, concentrated in Hong Kong office assets. Stripping out non-cash revaluation movements, recurring underlying profit surged 27% to a record, with dividends raised. The divergence between recurring cash earnings performance and statutory loss underscores how continued cap rate expansion on Hong Kong offices is overpowering operational improvements.
  • ADIA Anchors Dignari's Hong Kong-Focused Real Estate Private Credit Fund — Abu Dhabi Investment Authority, through a wholly owned subsidiary, committed capital on March 13 to Dignari Capital Partners' APAC Developed Markets Private Credit Strategy, the first significant sovereign wealth fund anchor for the vehicle. The strategy focuses on structured real estate lending across developed APAC, with Hong Kong as primary target, offering bridging and refinancing loans and capital for value-add conversions such as student housing and hotel upgrades across residential, office, retail, data centres and logistics. Dignari, led by former ADM Capital partner Grace Tan, had previously deployed a US$115 million private construction loan for a Sheung Wan office development.
  • GDS Holdings Turns Full-Year Profitable via Asset Monetisation — GDS reported full-year 2025 results on March 17, with revenue rising 10.8% YoY to RMB11.43 billion and net income of RMB959M—reversing a RMB770.9M net loss in 2024. The swing to profitability was driven by proceeds from ABS issuance and C-REIT listings rather than core operating improvement; Q4 alone recorded a net loss of RMB462.8M following a RMB1.56 billion non-cash impairment charge on data centres housed in leased properties with fixed remaining terms. Total 2025 new debt facilities raised reached RMB13.9 billion.
  • Korea’s 2026 assessed values trigger sharp tax increases – The Ministry of Land, Infrastructure and Transport released 2026 draft publicly assessed apartment prices on 17 March, showing nationwide increases of 9.16% and a 18.67% jump in Seoul, the third‑highest on record. High‑end districts such as Gangnam, Seocho and Songpa saw assessed values rise about 24–25%, pushing projected holding taxes for flagship complexes like Raemian One Bailey and Apgujeong Shin‑Hyundai up by more than 10 million won, or roughly 40–57% year‑on‑year. Homes above the 1.2 billion won comprehensive real estate tax threshold increased to about 487,000 units, over 85% of them in Seoul.
  • Tang Organization becomes new sponsor of Suntec REIT – On 17 March, Tang Organization’s wholly owned subsidiary Acrophyte Asset Management completed the acquisition of 100% of ESR Trust Management (Suntec) Limited, the manager of Suntec REIT. The deal makes Tang Organization the new sponsor, replacing ESR Group, and leaves the Tang family with roughly 36% of Suntec REIT’s units, the largest unitholder group. The manager and sponsor change follows an earlier unsuccessful takeover offer and consolidates economic and governance influence with the Tang interests.
